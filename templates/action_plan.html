{% elif hazard == 'cybersecurity' %}
Immediate (0-30 days)
  • Asset Inventory & Risk Assessment: Complete comprehensive inventory of all systems, devices, and software with access to PHI or public health data
  • Incident Response Plan: Activate cybersecurity incident response team and verify contact procedures for CISA (1-844-Say-CISA), HHS, and local law enforcement
  • Access Control Review: Implement immediate review of all user accounts, disable dormant accounts, and enforce multi-factor authentication for all critical systems
  • Business Continuity: Test offline backup procedures for surveillance systems (NNDSS, NSSP), electronic laboratory reporting (ELR), and electronic death records (EDR)
  • HIPAA Security Assessment: Conduct rapid assessment of PHI protection measures including encryption at rest and in transit
  • Staff Notification: Brief all staff on cybersecurity incident reporting procedures and current threat landscape
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • NIST Framework Implementation: Begin implementing NIST Cybersecurity Framework in compliance with CDC Additional Requirement 39
  • Surveillance System Contingencies: Develop manual reporting procedures for critical surveillance functions when electronic systems are compromised
  • Security Training Program: Implement quarterly cybersecurity awareness training including phishing simulation exercises for all staff
  • Network Segmentation: Isolate public health surveillance systems from general administrative networks to limit breach impact
  • Vendor Security Assessment: Review all business associate agreements and third-party vendor cybersecurity practices
  • Data Loss Prevention: Implement automated backup systems with offline storage and quarterly backup testing procedures
  • Vulnerability Management: Establish monthly vulnerability scanning and patch management procedures for all systems
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Information Sharing Architecture: Develop secure data exchange infrastructure with healthcare coalition partners, laboratories, and state/tribal health agencies
  • Threat Intelligence Program: Establish participation in health sector threat intelligence sharing networks and implement automated threat detection
  • Zero Trust Implementation: Implement zero trust network architecture for public health informatics systems and remote access
  • Cybersecurity Mutual Aid: Establish formal cybersecurity mutual aid agreements with regional health departments and state agencies
  • Advanced Monitoring: Deploy 24/7 security operations center (SOC) capabilities or managed security services for continuous monitoring
  • Compliance Program: Develop comprehensive HIPAA Security Rule compliance program incorporating 2025 regulatory updates
  • Resilience Testing: Conduct annual tabletop exercises and penetration testing to validate cybersecurity preparedness
  • Public Health Informatics Enhancement: Strengthen public health surveillance and informatics capabilities to support PHEP Capability 6 objectives
