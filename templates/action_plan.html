Based on the strategic risk assessment for {{ risk_data.location }}, this action plan provides structured guidance for annual public health preparedness planning. Implementation Steps
{% if hazard == 'flood' %}
Immediate (0-30 days)
  • Assess public health readiness for shelter health inspections and environmental health assessments at congregate care sites
  • Review medication continuity plans for populations likely to be displaced by flooding, including individuals on dialysis, insulin, or oxygen
  • Verify disease surveillance protocols for waterborne illness, wound infections, and vector-borne disease following flood events
  • Activate behavioral health crisis counseling plans and identify psychological first aid resources for flood-affected communities
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • Develop environmental health assessment procedures for flood-damaged properties including mold, private well contamination, and sewage exposure
  • Coordinate mass care health services with sheltering partners including chronic disease management and medical needs screening using the CMIST framework
  • Establish behavioral health support and crisis counseling referral networks for displaced populations and first responders
  • Create multilingual health communications on flood-related health risks including carbon monoxide poisoning, contaminated water, and mold exposure
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Build community health resilience through partnerships with behavioral health providers, aging and disability services, and social service agencies
  • Develop long-term behavioral health recovery framework for flood-affected populations including monitoring for post-traumatic stress
  • Conduct public health-focused flood response exercises with healthcare coalition partners emphasizing mass care and medical surge
  • Establish community health worker programs for ongoing health monitoring in flood-prone areas and populations with access and functional needs
{% elif hazard == 'tornado' %}
Immediate (0-30 days)
  • Assess shelter health inspection capacity and environmental health staffing for mass care operations at congregate sites
  • Develop protocols for chronic disease management and medication continuity for displaced populations including individuals with access and functional needs
  • Activate medical surge coordination with healthcare coalition partners for mass casualty incident response
  • Establish behavioral health crisis response teams and psychological first aid deployment procedures
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • Coordinate mass care health services including health screening, chronic disease management, and immunization services at shelters
  • Develop post-disaster environmental health assessment procedures for affected communities including food safety and sanitation
  • Establish medical countermeasure dispensing plans for tetanus prophylaxis and other post-disaster medical needs
  • Create culturally and linguistically appropriate health risk messaging for diverse populations in affected areas
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Develop long-term behavioral health recovery framework including grief counseling, substance use monitoring, and community resilience programs
  • Support fatality management operations through public health roles in death registration, family assistance centers, and behavioral health services for survivors
  • Establish medical records continuity systems and health care access plans for displaced populations
  • Build public health and healthcare coalition response capacity through joint exercises and after-action improvement planning
{% elif hazard == 'winter_storm' %}
Immediate (0-30 days)
  • Identify and map at-risk populations requiring medical equipment, refrigerated medications, or home-based care services vulnerable to power loss
  • Develop health communications for carbon monoxide poisoning prevention, hypothermia recognition, and fall injury prevention
  • Establish emergency protocols for medication delivery and durable medical equipment support for homebound individuals
  • Assess warming center capacity, accessibility, and health services availability for medically vulnerable and socially isolated populations
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • Coordinate with home healthcare providers and community health workers for wellness checks on medically vulnerable populations during extended cold events
  • Develop emergency transportation plans for dialysis patients, individuals requiring critical medical appointments, and medication-dependent populations
  • Establish healthcare coalition resource-sharing protocols and medical surge procedures for extended winter emergencies
  • Train community partners and Medical Reserve Corps volunteers on hypothermia and frostbite recognition and initial treatment
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Develop integrated health and social services winter preparedness plan with aging and disability services, behavioral health, and human services partners
  • Create emergency pharmaceutical and medical supply distribution systems for geographically isolated populations
  • Establish community health worker programs for year-round monitoring of medically vulnerable populations and pre-season wellness outreach
  • Build partnerships with faith-based organizations and volunteer networks for sheltering, wellness checks, and meal delivery during winter emergencies
{% elif hazard == 'active_shooter' %}

  • Assess medical surge capacity and healthcare coalition readiness for mass casualty events including trauma care coordination
  • Activate behavioral health crisis response planning and identify psychological first aid resources and crisis counseling providers
  • Review trauma-informed care protocols and establish crisis counseling referral networks for affected community members
  • Verify health communication plans for mass casualty incident public information and behavioral health resource messaging
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • Develop psychological first aid training for public health staff, community health workers, and Medical Reserve Corps volunteers
  • Establish trauma-informed behavioral health response protocols with healthcare coalition and community mental health partners
  • Create family reunification support procedures including health screening, crisis counseling, and behavioral health referrals
  • Train public health staff on Stop the Bleed hemorrhage control and coordinate with healthcare partners on mass casualty triage support

  • Build long-term behavioral health recovery services including trauma therapy access, substance use prevention, and community resilience programs
  • Establish community resilience programs with schools, faith-based organizations, and social service agencies for sustained recovery support
  • Conduct mass casualty medical surge and behavioral health exercises with healthcare coalition partners and community mental health providers
{% elif hazard == 'health' %}
Immediate (0-30 days)
  • Enhance syndromic surveillance systems and disease reporting mechanisms including electronic laboratory reporting capacity
  • Verify medical countermeasure dispensing plans including point of dispensing (POD) site readiness and closed POD partner agreements
  • Review nonpharmaceutical intervention protocols including isolation, quarantine, and social distancing implementation procedures
  • Assess community health needs and identify populations with health disparities, access barriers, and functional needs requiring targeted outreach
  • Activate health communication plans for disease outbreak messaging including pre-scripted materials for diverse community audiences
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • Establish bidirectional data sharing agreements with healthcare providers, laboratories, and state health department for disease reporting
  • Develop culturally appropriate vaccination and medical countermeasure outreach strategies for underserved and hard-to-reach populations
  • Update contact tracing protocols, case investigation procedures, and epidemiological investigation surge capacity plans
  • Train staff on laboratory specimen collection, packaging, and shipping procedures and coordinate with public health laboratory partners
  • Create multilingual health education materials on disease prevention, hygiene, and when to seek medical care
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Build community health resilience through partnerships with community health workers, social services, and faith-based organizations in underserved areas
  • Develop scalable disease surveillance and public health laboratory testing capacity for novel and emerging infectious diseases
  • Create comprehensive risk communication plans with pre-scripted messages and spokesperson training for diverse community audiences
  • Establish Medical Reserve Corps recruitment and training programs for mass dispensing, vaccination, and epidemiological investigation support
  • Conduct infectious disease response exercises testing POD operations, contact tracing surge, and nonpharmaceutical intervention implementation
{% elif hazard == 'cybersecurity' %}
Immediate (0-30 days)
  • Asset Inventory and Risk Assessment: Complete comprehensive inventory of all systems, devices, and software with access to PHI or public health data
  • Incident Response Plan: Activate cybersecurity incident response team and verify contact procedures for CISA (1-844-Say-CISA), HHS, and local law enforcement
  • Access Control Review: Implement immediate review of all user accounts, disable dormant accounts, and enforce multi-factor authentication for all critical systems
  • Business Continuity: Test offline backup procedures for surveillance systems (NNDSS, NSSP), electronic laboratory reporting (ELR), and electronic death records (EDR)
  • HIPAA Security Assessment: Conduct rapid assessment of PHI protection measures including encryption at rest and in transit
  • Staff Notification: Brief all staff on cybersecurity incident reporting procedures and current threat landscape
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • NIST Framework Implementation: Begin implementing NIST Cybersecurity Framework in compliance with CDC Additional Requirement 39
  • Surveillance System Contingencies: Develop manual reporting procedures for critical surveillance functions when electronic systems are compromised
  • Security Training Program: Implement quarterly cybersecurity awareness training including phishing simulation exercises for all staff
  • Network Segmentation: Isolate public health surveillance systems from general administrative networks to limit breach impact
  • Vendor Security Assessment: Review all business associate agreements and third-party vendor cybersecurity practices
  • Data Loss Prevention: Implement automated backup systems with offline storage and quarterly backup testing procedures
  • Vulnerability Management: Establish monthly vulnerability scanning and patch management procedures for all systems
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Information Sharing Architecture: Develop secure data exchange infrastructure with healthcare coalition partners, laboratories, and state/tribal health agencies
  • Threat Intelligence Program: Establish participation in health sector threat intelligence sharing networks and implement automated threat detection
  • Zero Trust Implementation: Implement zero trust network architecture for public health informatics systems and remote access
  • Cybersecurity Mutual Aid: Establish formal cybersecurity mutual aid agreements with regional health departments and state agencies
  • Advanced Monitoring: Deploy 24/7 security operations center (SOC) capabilities or managed security services for continuous monitoring
  • Compliance Program: Develop comprehensive HIPAA Security Rule compliance program incorporating 2025 regulatory updates
  • Resilience Testing: Conduct annual tabletop exercises and penetration testing to validate cybersecurity preparedness
  • Public Health Informatics Enhancement: Strengthen public health surveillance and informatics capabilities to support PHEP Capability 6 objectives
{% elif hazard == 'extreme_heat' %}
Immediate (0-30 days)
  • Map high-risk populations using social vulnerability indices, chronic disease registries, and housing data to identify heat-vulnerable communities
  • Develop heat response activation criteria and public health department operating procedures including staffing and resource deployment
  • Establish syndromic surveillance for heat-related illness through emergency department data sharing and poison control coordination
  • Assess cooling center accessibility for older adults, persons with disabilities, outdoor workers, and transportation-dependent populations
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • Develop multilingual heat health communication materials addressing risks for diverse communities, outdoor workers, and populations experiencing homelessness
  • Create coordinated wellness check outreach systems with aging services, home healthcare providers, and community health workers for older adults and socially isolated residents
  • Train healthcare providers, community health workers, and Medical Reserve Corps volunteers on recognition and treatment of heat-related illness
  • Establish behavioral health monitoring protocols for heat-related stress and crisis counseling needs in vulnerable populations
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Develop heat vulnerability reduction strategies with community health partners, social services, and housing agencies to address root causes
  • Create health equity initiatives to address disproportionate heat impacts in underserved communities and populations with chronic health conditions
  • Establish cooling center networks integrated with health services, medication management, and behavioral health support
  • Build community health worker programs for pre-season outreach and ongoing heat season monitoring in high-risk neighborhoods
{% elif hazard == 'air_quality' %}
Immediate (0-30 days)
  • Establish health alert protocols linked to air quality index forecasts and wildfire smoke advisories for populations with respiratory and cardiovascular conditions
  • Develop health guidance and activity modification recommendations for vulnerable populations including individuals with asthma, COPD, and cardiovascular disease
  • Identify clean air shelter locations with enhanced filtration for extended smoke events and assess accessibility for individuals with access and functional needs
  • Activate syndromic surveillance for respiratory illness and asthma exacerbations during poor air quality episodes
Short-term (1-3 months)
  • Create multilingual public health messaging for different air quality severity levels with targeted guidance for sensitive groups
  • Develop targeted outreach for medically vulnerable populations during extended smoke events including medication management and wellness checks
  • Coordinate with schools, childcare facilities, and outdoor recreation programs on indoor air quality guidance and activity modification protocols
  • Establish health screening and respiratory health monitoring protocols for community members with pre-existing conditions during extended events
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Build community health resilience programs for recurring air quality events with emphasis on populations with chronic respiratory and cardiovascular conditions
  • Develop epidemiological monitoring and health impact analysis capacity for air quality-related illness trends over time
  • Establish behavioral health support resources for communities affected by extended smoke events and associated anxiety or displacement
  • Create community health worker programs for respiratory health education and asthma management support in high-risk communities

  • Activate waterborne disease surveillance protocols for potential flood-related contamination of private wells and community water systems

  • Create healthcare coalition coordination protocols for patient tracking, medical needs assessment, and chronic disease care continuity
  • Establish environmental health monitoring procedures for post-storm water quality testing, food safety inspections, and vector control assessment
  • Train community health workers and Medical Reserve Corps volunteers on storm-related health risks and post-event wellness check procedures
Long-term (3-12 months)
  • Develop behavioral health support services for storm-affected communities including crisis counseling and post-disaster mental health monitoring
  • Build community health resilience through partnerships with social services, behavioral health providers, and aging and disability services
  • Establish health facility continuity of operations plans for recurrent severe weather events including backup power and supply chain procedures
  • Create targeted wellness check programs for medically vulnerable populations during severe weather season with community partner coordination
