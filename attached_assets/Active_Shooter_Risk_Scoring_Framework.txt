
# Active Shooter Risk Assessment Scoring Framework

## Purpose:
This scoring framework is intended to support public health and public safety planning
by identifying environmental and social indicators correlated with elevated risk conditions
for active shooter events.

## Scoring Domains & Indicators

1. Historical Incident Density (Weight: 25%)
   - Indicator: Past active shooter or gun violence incidents (last 10 years)
   - Source: FBI Active Shooter Reports, Gun Violence Archive
   - Scoring: Normalize per 100k population by county or metro area

2. School & Youth Vulnerability (Weight: 20%)
   - Indicators: School incident reports, bullying prevalence, youth disconnectedness
   - Source: NCES, CRDC, ACS
   - Scoring: Composite index from school environment and youth risk indicators

3. Social & Community Fragility (Weight: 20%)
   - Indicators: Isolation, single households, hate crimes, community trauma
   - Source: CDC SVI, FBI Hate Crime Reports
   - Scoring: Average of SVI (social cohesion), hate crime rate, and isolation metrics

4. Mental Health & Behavioral Health Risk (Weight: 20%)
   - Indicators: Poor mental health days, access to providers, reported distress
   - Source: County Health Rankings, BRFSS, HRSA
   - Scoring: Normalize values and invert access score

5. Access to Lethal Means (Weight: 15%)
   - Indicators: Firearm ownership laws, storage practices, UCR gun-related crimes
   - Source: RAND Firearm Law Database, CDC WISQARS
   - Scoring: Based on permissiveness of state laws + proxy gun prevalence

## Scoring Method:
- Each domain produces a 0.0 to 1.0 score (normalized)
- Total Risk Score = Weighted sum of domain scores (rounded to 2 decimals)

## Ethical Guidelines:
- Use only aggregate, anonymized data
- Do not use for profiling individuals
- Intended for public safety awareness, program targeting, and prevention strategy
