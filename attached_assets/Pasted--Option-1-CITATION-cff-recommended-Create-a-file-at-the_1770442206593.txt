✅ Option 2: “How to Cite CARA” (README section)

📚 How to Cite CARA

If you use CARA (Comprehensive Automated Risk Assessment) in academic research, policy analysis, reports, or operational planning documents, please cite it as software.

Recommended citation (APA-style)

Niedermeier, J. (2026). CARA: Comprehensive Automated Risk Assessment (Version 2.3) [Software]. https://github.com/jdn63/CARA

BibTeX
@software{cara_2026,
  author  = {Niedermeier, Jaime},
  title   = {CARA: Comprehensive Automated Risk Assessment},
  year    = {2026},
  version = {2.3},
  url     = {https://github.com/jdn63/CARA}
}

Acknowledgment guidance

When used in operational or policy contexts, users are encouraged to acknowledge CARA as:

“An open-source public health preparedness risk assessment tool developed for jurisdiction-level strategic planning.”

✅ Optional (but strong): Ethics-aware citation note

This is optional, but very aligned with CARA’s values:

CARA is intended to support preparedness planning, risk awareness, and policy analysis. It should not be used as the sole basis for real-time emergency response or enforcement decisions without corroborating authoritative sources.
